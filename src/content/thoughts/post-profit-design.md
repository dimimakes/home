# Post-Profit Design V1

We live in a for-profit society. Most everything we design is made to compete with comparable things within a for-profit market system. The device you are reading this text on is a good example. Ask yourself: Why does your device exist? What factors determined and shaped its features, the way it's marketed and uses it affords? If we consider these questions, the answer becomes clear: profit. A company made your device so they can sell it for more than it cost them to make. Profit is the principle of our economies, and the benchmark against which all decisions are made (I'll save the exceptions for another time). A good question to ask is where does a profit-normative society lead us, and what kind of behaviors does it incentivize?

Given the rising wealth inequality, wars, and the climate crisis, it may be high time to consider alternatives. We've tried capitalism, and we've tried centralized planning. What else is there? A few months ago I came across an economic system called "The Participatory Economy Model". For a detailed, yet digestible explanation of the system, I suggest checking out this website: [https://participatoryeconomy.org/](https://participatoryeconomy.org/)

In short, imagine we have no more markets, no more companies and no more possibility to earn money through owned assets. Instead we have collectives, shared ownership, and earning money only through one's own labor. How might such a system work? And how might we design technologies within such a system? This is Post-Profit Design: Designing technologies within a participatory economy. My aim is to combine Research Through Design, Participatory Design, and Speculative Design to explore what Post-Profit Design could look like; what design principles and benchmarks is it characterized by, and if desirable, how might we transition to such a system over time.

## Key Takeaways:

- Our current society is profit-normative, and the ability to make a profit determines what we design and what we do (for the most part).
- The for-profit system leads to wealth inequality, wars, and a climate crisis, making it worthwhile to rethink the system's incentives.
- Post-Profit Design is a different system where designing is done in alignment with a participatory economy system, where collectives and earning only through one's labor characterize society.
